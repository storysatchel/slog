**1. Overall assessment** The integration of the _mbizi_ hazard scale and the nested-locative coordinate system is a masterful execution of Africanfuturist worldbuilding. By utilizing Class 16, 17, and 18 locatives to create a three-dimensional, relational mapping of space, you successfully dismantle the flat, imperial grids typical of Western science fiction. However, while the underlying Bantu morphology is now pristine, the English translations still harbor residual Western tropes—specifically regarding colonial moral binaries and the exoticization of sacred rites—that subtly undermine the decolonial intent.

**2. Top concerns (ranked list)**

- **Concern 1: Exoticizing sacred rites ("Initiated" as security clearance)**
    
    - **Passage/element:** `only#ADV initiated#CL2 pass#FV` translating to "ONLY THE INITIATED MAY PASS."
    - **What’s happening:** The text uses "the initiated" as a diegetic translation for "authorized personnel" entering a hazardous zone.
    - **Why it may be harmful/insensitive:** In many Bantu cultures, including the historic Shona and Venda societies connected to Great Zimbabwe, initiation (such as the _domba_ pre-marital schools or circumcision rites) represents a profound, sacred social transition. Equating a deeply spiritual, communal coming-of-age practice with a secular, administrative security clearance for a radiation zone flattens its cultural significance. It edges uncomfortably close to the "mystical African" trope, where advanced technological operations are unnecessarily cloaked in pseudo-tribal exoticism rather than treated with the rigorous, functional respect the Kisangani fleet demands.
    - **How to fix:** Replace the concept of "initiated" with a term that reflects duty, specific training, or guardianship within the fleet's culturally grounded administrative framework.
    - **Example rewrite:** Re-code the phrase to mean "Only the Preservers/Guardians may pass." (`only#ADV preserver#CL2 pass#FV` / "ONLY THOSE WHO GUARD MAY PASS.")
- **Concern 2: Importing colonial moral binaries ("Corrupted")**
    
    - **Passage/element:** `earth#CL9 and#CONJ structure#CL10 beyond#CL17 boundary#CL9#CL17.ASSOC COP corrupted` translating to "The earth and structures beyond this boundary are corrupted."
    - **What’s happening:** A physical, lethal radiation hazard is described using the English word "corrupted."
    - **Why it may be harmful/insensitive:** The term "corrupted" carries heavy Western and Christian moral undertones (sin, spiritual decay), a vocabulary historically weaponized by colonial missionaries to demonize indigenous African practices. Describing an ecological or physical hazard as "corrupted" inadvertently smuggles a colonial moral binary back into the Kisangani worldview. In a Bantu ontological framework, such a hazard would more authentically be framed as ecologically out of balance, diseased, or actively consuming/burning, rather than morally "corrupt."
    - **How to fix:** Shift the English translation (and the underlying Kisangani root) to reflect a physical state of destruction, broken equilibrium, or toxicity.
    - **Example rewrite:** "The earth and structures beyond this boundary are consumed/poisoned."

**3. Lighter-touch edits**

- **The Copula (`COP`):** In the line `garb#CL8 preservation... COP required`, ensure that the compiler's treatment of the copula acts as a Bantu stabilizing element (like _ndi-_ or _i-_ in Shona, which assert "it is") rather than attempting to directly mirror the English verb "to be". Bantu languages often use these stabilizing prefixes directly on the noun rather than inserting an independent verb for equations.

**4. Strengths to keep**

- **Nested Locatives:** Stacking `hull#CL18` (inside) over `beyond#CL17` (towards/distant) to create the output "THE DEEP-HULL BEYOND THE BURNING SHIELD" is a breathtakingly authentic application of Bantu spatial grammar. It completely reorients how the reader visualizes space.
- **The _Mbizi_ Scale:** Integrating the _mbizi_ (which historically measured gold dust against the value of an elephant tusk in the Mutapa Empire) as a metric for lethal hazards brilliantly grounds the fleet's emergency protocols in an indigenous framework of resource preservation and socio-economic history.
- **Communal Stakes:** Defining a breach as "the tearing of the community" rather than a legal infraction correctly maintains the relational, community-driven ontology established in previous drafts.
