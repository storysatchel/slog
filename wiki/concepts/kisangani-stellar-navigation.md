# Kisangani Stellar Navigation

## Translating the *Dau* to the Stars
The Kisangani fleet's approach to stellar navigation draws directly from the profound maritime legacy of the Swahili Coast and Kilwa Kisiwani, dynamically mapped onto the hard sci-fi constraints of *The Expanse*. 

In historical coastal navigation, there was a stark division in technology based on the environment. This division translates perfectly to the deep-space reality of the fleet:

1. **The Mutumbwi (Intra-Fleet Shuttles):** Historically used for navigating the calm, shallow inland seas, the term has been phonologically assimilated into Kisangani Basic as *mutumbwi* (Class 3, plural *mitumbwi*). These represent the small, agile shuttles used exclusively in the "calm" localized space within a single ship's wake or between tightly clustered habitats.
2. **The Dau (Capital Ships):** Historically equipped with revolutionary keel structures for the rough open ocean, the *dau* (plural *madau*, Class 5/6) designates the massive capital ships of the fleet. These vessels utilize advanced hydrodynamic energy-keels to ride the "rough" solar winds and gravitational waves of deep space.

By assimilating these loanwords (e.g., ensuring *mtumbwi* becomes *mutumbwi* to satisfy the strict Shona C-V syllabic structure and Class 3/4 *mu-/mi-* prefixes), the fleet honors its pan-African heritage without flattening the linguistic reality into a disjointed mashup.

## The Vatasvimhepo (The Wind-Riders)
Historically, the term *Waungwana* ("the freeborn" or "patricians") denoted the noble maritime culture of the Swahili city-states. However, uncritically adopting this term risks sanitizing a history of rigid class hierarchy and Indian Ocean exploitation. Africanfuturism demands that we reclaim authentic history without romanticizing its oppressive structures.

The Kisangani fleet has discarded the stratified *Waungwana* identity. Instead, the navigators who chart the solar winds and map the *chizivo* are known as the **Vatasvimhepo** ("the Wind-Riders", derived from the Shona roots *tasva*, to ride, and *mhepo*, wind). They are not an elite patrician class, but an egalitarian guild whose authority stems from their rigorous communal labor and navigational expertise.
