# Computational Decolonization

Computational decolonization is the practice of designing technical systems, programming languages, and operating systems that natively reflect non-Western epistemologies and ontologies, resisting the imposition of Western/capitalist defaults like Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) or Boolean binaries.

## How It Works
By embedding indigenous philosophical principles (such as Bantu ontology) directly into the logic gates and data structures, technology ceases to be an assimilating force and instead becomes a culturally sustaining architecture.
