# Expanse Diegetic Signage and Xi-Mallow 434 Lore Constraints

## Context
During the generation of English-only diegetic signage for future conlang translation efforts (e.g., Belter Creole, Eridian Basic, and Kisangani Bantu/Shona blends), a continuity review was conducted on the hazard markings for the Xi-Mallow 434 quarantine zone.

## Finding / Decision
Signage across different Expanse locations must reflect their specific cultural and political landscapes:
- **Government Documents:** Incorporate bureaucratic and decolonial themes (e.g., Kisangani trade agreements using placeholders for *uzororo* and *ruhanda*).
- **Hazard Markings (Xi-Mallow 434):** The external quarantine perimeter is enforced by the **United Nations Navy (UNN)**, not the Mars Congressional Republic (MCR). External warning signs must use standard UNN iconography, keeping ancient Martian sigils hidden within the vault. The stated "99.9% mortality rate" from biological hazards is a fabricated UNN cover story to deter prospectors.
- **Physical Realism:** Asteroids of Xi-Mallow 434's size (vulcanoid marble) lack an atmosphere. Hazard warnings must correctly reference contamination in the "regolith and subterranean structures."

## Reasoning
The UNN has maintained a secret quarantine over Xi-Mallow 434 for 160 years. While the vault contents are early Martian in origin, the external authority enforcing the blockade is Earth's UNN. Maintaining hard-sci-fi physics requires accurately reflecting the vacuum-exposed nature of small asteroids.

## Implications
Future conlang translations for Martian hazard markings must carefully distinguish between ancient Martian artifacts (which feature traditional silver and blue circle-and-arrow symbols and Chinese ideograms) and modern enforcement warnings (which must align with UNN protocols).
